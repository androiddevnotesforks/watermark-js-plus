## Design Context

### Users

Developers and testers using the watermark-js-plus documentation to configure, test, and decode browser watermarks. On the decode page, they need to provide a screenshot or image quickly without leaving their current workflow.

### Brand Personality

Clear, technical, and trustworthy. The interface should feel native to the existing VitePress and Element Plus documentation rather than like a separate application.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the Element Plus upload interaction as the visual reference, including a focused drag-and-drop surface with click-to-select support. Preserve the project's blue accent and support both VitePress light and dark themes. Keep the upload flow compact and task-oriented.

### Design Principles

- Make drag, click, and clipboard paste equally discoverable.
- Keep upload state, image preview, and replacement actions in one coherent surface.
- Reuse Element Plus behavior and theme variables for consistency and accessibility.
- Localize all visible instructions for the English and Chinese documentation.
- Prefer restrained feedback and clear hierarchy over decorative effects.
